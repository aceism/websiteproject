1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright is something that can affect websites, an example of this is when an image/video/music is used by the website owner even though it is not originally theirs e.g. not taken by them. This means if the original photo/video owner comes across their image/video/music being used on a site without their permission they could ask them to take it down and could even take legal action if they believe its the right thing to do. A way to avoid that situation is to use images/videos/music that are copyright free or use your own content therefore it is legally yours to use.

Another constraint is sometimes layouts on websites can differ between devices e.g. computer and mobile. The website might not have been designed with a mobile layout in mind even though a majority of the population only use their phones to browse online. People should now really consider creating a seperate design specifically for phones e.g. iPhone and Android

1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account

When it comes to websites there can be some issues when using them, this is to be expected but some could be changed and fixed.

For example if people have difficulty with their vision you could make sure that the text is bigger on the pages more than the default 11 or 12 font size. There is also options to have the text spoken out loud, which would be useful for people who are blind and want to be able to know the content of your website, and if you write a description for your images they can be read out as well to describe the visual elements.

1.7 Describe which are the appropriate filetypes for websites

There are filetypes that are used to make websites appear correctly on PC's/Devices. HMTL and CSS are used during the website creation and the pages need to be saved with these typings. PNG and JPEG are used for images, MP3 is used for audio and MP4 is used for videos.
